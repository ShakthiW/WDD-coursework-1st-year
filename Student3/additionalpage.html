Football clubs can earn money from a variety of sources, including:
  1. Matchday revenue: This includes income from ticket sales, hospitality packages, and merchandise sales during home matches.
  2. Broadcasting rights: Football clubs can earn significant income from broadcasting rights, such as television and streaming deals, for their matches. The value of these rights can vary depending on the league, the popularity of the team, and the geographic location of the audience.
  3. Sponsorship deals: Many football clubs have sponsorships with companies who pay to have their branding featured on the team's jerseys, in the stadium, or on other promotional materials.
  4. Transfer fees: Football clubs can earn money from the sale of players to other teams. If a player is developed within the club's academy, the club can also receive a percentage of any future transfer fees if the player is sold to another team.
  5. Other commercial activities: Some football clubs may also earn revenue from other commercial activities, such as hosting concerts or events at the stadium, or renting out space for conferences or other gatherings.
